# Telos (Idealized Form)

`in-midst-my-life` is conceived as a sovereign identity presentation medium based on the **Inverted Interview** paradigm.

## The Core Thesis
Conventional hiring structures place the applicant in a passive, submissive posture: broadcasting a singular, static CV to multiple recipients while performing standardized applications.

The Inverted Interview upends this power dynamic:
1. **Interactive Inversion**: The prospective employer or partner must first state their own values, role context, requirements, and culture by interacting with an interface.
2. **Contextual Disclosure**: Instead of showing a generalized resume, the candidate's identity ledger dynamically projects a curated, weighted, and filtered representation of their life achievements (a *mask* or *lens*) that resonates specifically with the inquirer's domain and needs.
3. **Formal Integrity**: All credentials, accomplishments, and career milestones are formally modeled, cryptographic-ready, and categorized to verify capabilities while protecting the candidate's agency and cognitive style.

## Theoretical Grounding
- **Hermeneutic Presentation**: The presentation of self is not a single document but a multiplexed set of *Personae* or *Masks* (e.g., Artisan, Synthesist, Architect, Scholar) that translate underlying professional records into the vocabulary of the recipient.
- **The Hunter Protocol**: A specialized interface for actively matching candidate capacity to industry opportunities, modeling compatibility across multiple axes.
- **The Academic Domain**: Proving compatibility and research impact using bibliometrics, citation analysis, and scholar-to-position mapping without central intermediaries.
